Developer, Designer, Leader.

“Leadership is the capacity to translate vision into reality.” —Warren Bennis

Talented professionals deserve better than being cogs in a machine. While many companies still operate that way, I say that if you can't beat them, don't join them: change the game.

Experience and research have taught me the value of culture in getting outstanding people to deliver outstanding results. It takes more than looking for skilled “resources” or providing great perks. It’s about finding people with high emotional intelligence, and making people-centric decisions that set them up for success.

Over the last four years, I have focused increasingly on three areas: promoting challenging work that drives skills development (rather than just rushing to deliver); building amazing relationships (internally and externally); and helping others achieve success (based on their definition). These areas align with the three factors I believe generate amazing work cultures: the acquisition, retention, and empowerment of outstanding people.

These factors, and the culture that results from them, exist at the intersection of freedom and responsibility. Exceptional, responsible individuals want to achieve great results, and care a great deal about the process. It is on us as leaders to create an environment that enables our teams to achieve greatness.

Creating such a culture for my team drives the way I work, the initiatives I champion internally, and the way I seek to grow professionally.
